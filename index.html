About Me & The Church

My name is Kaleb Snarr, and I am from South Jordan, Utah. I am a missionary for my church. When you think about Utah, one of the first things that may come to mind is "Mormons". You might be wondering, what is a Mormon? Well, that's exactly what I'm here to explain. A "Mormon" is someone who is a member of the "Mormon" religion. A couple of things that members of the Church believe include:

  • Jesus Christ, God, & The Holy Ghost
  • Baptism
  • Church Attendance
  • Prayer
  • Priesthood
  • Scripture

The term "Mormon" is often used to describe the members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (which is the correct term). The members of this church believe in Jesus Christ as our Savior and Redeemer. We believe in God (our Heavenly Father) and the Holy Ghost, as well as the Bible and the Book of Mormon as scripture and doctrine. Additionally, we follow prophets, one of whom was Joseph Smith, who found and translated the Book of Mormon.

Our Purpose As Missionaries

Our Mission

Our goal is to seek out people who are willing to learn about the gospel that we care so much about. It has brought me happiness, thus I want to offer the opportunity for others to feel the same.

Travel & Time

As missionaries, we have the opportunity to leave our homes when we turn 18 (or 19 for women) for a period of 18 months to 2 years to share the gospel we believe in. We are assigned to a place, which could be almost anywhere in the world. For example, I was called to the Santo Domingo East Mission in the Dominican Republic.

Spread Love

As missionaries, we are taught to have love for everyone around us. We are expected to be kind and to treat others and their cultures with respect as we pursue our goal of teaching the gospel of Jesus Christ. This is one of the most important aspects of being a missionary.

Study

Studying is a major part of being a missionary. We receive training and attend classes before leaving for our mission locations. We are expected to be very knowledgeable about the gospel so we can teach effectively. Sometimes, we are also expected to learn a new language for the purpose of teaching.

Service

Another significant aspect of serving a mission is engaging in service. By "service", we mean assisting the communities in which we reside during our missions. We find joy in helping others because it allows us to exemplify what we aim to be, which is like Jesus.

Listen & Learn

Our job is to teach, but it's also to learn. As a missionary, we are very open to cultures that we are unfamiliar with, people we are unfamiliar with, and places we are unfamiliar with. We want to teach, but we want to learn just as much!
